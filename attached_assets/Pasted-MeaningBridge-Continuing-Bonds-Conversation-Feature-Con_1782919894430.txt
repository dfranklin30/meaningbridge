MeaningBridge Continuing Bonds Conversation Feature

Concept Summary

The feature allows a grieving user to create a personalized “continuing bond” profile for a deceased loved one, so they can later have guided therapeutic conversations that feel emotionally familiar, meaningful, and grounded in memory.

Each user may create multiple loved-one profiles, since people can experience more than one loss. Each profile stores information about the deceased person’s voice, personality, beliefs, memories, phrases, relationships, and emotional significance. The AI then uses that profile to support conversations that resemble the person’s style while remaining clearly framed as a therapeutic grief-support experience, not a literal replacement for the person.

The goal is not just open-ended chatting. The larger vision is to provide guided conversations based on grief therapy approaches, especially continuing bonds theory and Dr. Robert Neimeyer’s meaning reconstruction work. Users may eventually choose conversation types such as final words, forgiveness, gratitude, unresolved questions, reassurance, legacy, or saying goodbye.

Core User Flow

User opens the Continuing Bonds section.
User creates a loved-one profile.
User optionally uploads a photo or selects an icon/avatar.
User answers setup prompts about the loved one.
User may add writing samples, text messages, emails, phrases, nicknames, memories, beliefs, and personality traits.
The profile is saved and editable later.
When the user wants a conversation, they select the loved one from a list of profile cards.
The AI loads that person’s profile into the conversation context.
The user chooses either:
Open conversation
Guided therapeutic conversation
Final conversation
Forgiveness conversation
Gratitude conversation
Legacy conversation
Unfinished business conversation
The AI conducts the conversation in a gentle, supportive, grief-informed way.

Setup Modes

Basic Setup

A quick version for users who are emotionally overwhelmed. This should ask only a few gentle questions:

Who are you creating this profile for?
What was your relationship?
What did they call you?
What are a few phrases they used?
What do you miss most about them?
What kind of conversation would help you right now?

Expanded Setup

A deeper version for users who want a more accurate and emotionally rich profile:

Upload writing samples
Add text messages or emails
Add memories
Add religious/spiritual beliefs
Add personality traits
Add family context
Add unfinished topics
Add things the loved one would never say
Add preferred therapeutic conversation goals

Conversation Types

Open Continuing Bond Conversation

A gentle, emotionally supportive conversation where the user can speak freely.

Final Conversation

For users who did not get to say goodbye or want one more meaningful exchange.

Forgiveness Conversation

For guilt, regret, conflict, or unresolved pain.

Gratitude Conversation

For expressing love, appreciation, and legacy.

Unfinished Business Conversation

For things left unsaid, unresolved conflicts, or unanswered questions.

Legacy Conversation

For remembering what the person taught, valued, and passed on.

Guided Meaning Reconstruction Conversation

Based on grief therapy principles, helping the user integrate the loss into their continuing life story.

Safety and Ethical Guardrails

The AI should never claim to actually be the deceased person. It should be framed as a therapeutic reconstruction based on the user’s memories and provided materials.

Suggested disclaimer:
“This conversation is created from your memories and the details you provided. It is not your loved one, but a reflective support tool to help you continue your bond, express what matters, and process grief.”

The AI should:

Stay gentle and nonjudgmental.
Avoid making new factual claims about the deceased.
Avoid manipulative or overly certain language.
Avoid saying “I am really here” or “I never died.”
Encourage professional support if the user expresses severe distress, self-harm, or inability to function.
Allow users to pause, exit, or save the conversation.

AI Prompting Approach

The system should combine:

A safety and ethics prompt.
The selected therapeutic conversation mode.
The loved-one profile.
The user’s current emotional goal.
Instructions to speak in a style inspired by the loved one’s remembered voice, without claiming to literally be them.

Example AI instruction:
“You are facilitating a continuing bonds grief-support conversation. Speak in a voice inspired by the user’s memories of [Name], using only the traits, phrases, values, and memories provided. Do not claim to literally be the deceased person. Keep the tone gentle, grounded, emotionally supportive, and aligned with grief therapy principles.”
